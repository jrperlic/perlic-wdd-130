Elevator Pitch

Here’s my idea. I want to make a website promoting a fictitious chess club/team. The site would have a few articles, a record (results and pictures) of the players’ games, a page for how to join the team, and maybe a page for coaching. This fictitious chess club/team is highly competitive and goes to national, and even some international, tournaments, so the “selling point” is that if you live nearby you can join/get coaching/learn tips from some of the top players in the nation. But it would not really be for profit, just a platform for the chess club/team.

Purpose Statement

We would like to create an online presence for our chess team in the form of a website. The purpose of the site would be to indirectly build revenue for the team, provide information about our chess team, and share a love for the game of chess with people across the world. The site would build revenue by raising awareness for chess tournaments and offering coaching to customers. We would also share information about our team and offer tips and advice to chess enthusiasts. It would be a hub for chess masters to share advice and chess players to find information about chess.

Audience Statement

The target customers are people interested in chess, and even people who do not yet know anything about the game. Their lives and situations would be varied, but prospective chess team members would have to live in the same geographical area. The customers will want to join the team, or they might want to get a chess coach, or to find nearby tournaments. Their needs are not being adequately met right now because there is no chess club in their area, or they do not have a coach, or there are no tournaments nearby. The audience will access the website via their desktop or laptop computer.

Mission Statement

Our mission is to share a love for the game of chess; to help chess enthusiasts learn to be better players; to provide players with coaching; and to host tournaments for players in our geographical area. We operate in the western United States (haven’t decided exactly where yet), but we have teams all over the world. We value teamwork, strategy, and integrity, and we believe that the best way to compete is as a team. We envision a world where everyone plays chess, whether competitively or for fun, and we hope to be a part of that future world—sharing in games of chess and teaching others what makes the game so fun!
